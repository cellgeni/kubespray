Human Embryonic Hindlimb

The human limb starts as a small bud before going through a complex series of cellular events to create the final limb. How these limb bud cells orchestrate limb organogenesis in response to gene expression and cellular interactions remains poorly understood. Here, we analyzed the transcriptomes of 27,426 cells from 5 human embryonic limbs between 5 and 8 weeks post-conception to build a high-resolution single-cell transcriptome atlas of human embryonic limb development.
